\begin{tablenotes}[flushleft]
\footnotesize
\item \textit{Notes.} Each row reports stability statistics from a
leave-one-year-out cross-validation that re-estimates the parent specification
fifteen times, dropping one calendar year from the in-sample training window
(January~2005 through December~2019) on each fold. The estimation specification
matches the in-sample inference exactly, with two-way (entity and time) fixed
effects and entity-clustered standard errors, restricted to the 2005--2019
training window. The Same-sign share is the percentage of folds in which the
re-estimated coefficient retains the same sign as the full-sample (no-fold-out)
estimate on the same window. The $5\%$ sig.\ share is the percentage of folds
in which the re-estimated coefficient is significant at the $5\%$ level under
entity-clustered standard errors. Full-sample and median coefficients are
reported on the 2005--2019 training window used by the LOO exercise. They
may differ in magnitude from the corresponding figures in
Tables~\ref{tab:main_results} and~\ref{tab:extended_results}, which are
estimated on the full 2005--February~2026 sample. The reported stability
properties (same-sign and significance shares) are interpretable directly
and do not depend on the magnitude offset. Variables not listed here either
are not flagged for stability scrutiny by the Driscoll--Kraay exercise in
Table~\ref{tab:dk_robustness} or do not have a sufficient role in the
headline narrative to warrant fold-level reporting.
\end{tablenotes}
