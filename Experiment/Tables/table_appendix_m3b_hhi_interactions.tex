\begin{tablenotes}[flushleft]
\footnotesize
\item \textit{Notes.} Communication is the omitted reference sector. Each row
reports the differential HHI loading for the named sector relative to
Communication, identified by the interaction term $D_j \times HHI_{j,t}$ in
equations~\eqref{eq:model3a} and~\eqref{eq:model3b}. The M3A column repeats
the interactions from Table~\ref{tab:net_sector_effects} for direct comparison.
$t$-statistics in parentheses, entity-clustered. Significance: $^{*}\,p<0.10$,
$^{**}\,p<0.05$, $^{***}\,p<0.01$.
\item The Financials HHI interaction reverses sign between M3A ($+0.039$,
$t=0.51$, statistically indistinguishable from the omitted Communication
baseline) and M3B ($-0.365$, $t=-2.86$). This sign-flip is consistent with
multicollinearity between the heterogeneous HHI and heterogeneous Supplier HHI
columns when both are included simultaneously. Within Financials, internal
HHI and Supplier HHI co-move strongly across the sample, so the M3B saturated
specification distributes a single underlying loading across two collinear
regressors and produces an unstable point estimate on each. The Health Care
HHI interaction also attenuates substantially in M3B ($+0.034$, $t=0.44$)
relative to M3A ($+0.163$, $t=3.18$), and the Health Care Supplier HHI
interaction in M3B is correspondingly large and significant
($+0.195$, $t=3.01$ in main Table~\ref{tab:extended_results}); the same
multicollinearity logic plausibly applies. The seven other HHI sector
interactions are stable in sign and magnitude between M3A and M3B.
\item This table is referenced from Section~5.3.2 of the main text and from
the footer of main Table~\ref{tab:extended_results}.
\end{tablenotes}
